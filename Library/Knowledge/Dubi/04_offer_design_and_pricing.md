# Topic 4: Offer Design & Pricing Strategy

*Make offers so good people feel stupid saying no.*

---

## The Value Equation (Hormozi)

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
        -------------------------------------------------------
              (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

| Variable | Position | Goal | Lever |
|----------|----------|------|-------|
| Dream Outcome | Numerator | MAXIMIZE | Make it specific, emotional, measurable |
| Perceived Likelihood | Numerator | MAXIMIZE | Proof, testimonials, guarantees, case studies |
| Time Delay | Denominator | MINIMIZE | Faster results = higher perceived value |
| Effort & Sacrifice | Denominator | MINIMIZE | Less work for the customer = more value |

**How to use it:**
1. Score each variable 1-10 from the customer's perspective
2. Fix the weakest variable first — that's your biggest value leak
3. Compare against competitors on all four variables
4. The offer should deliver at minimum 10x the value of the price

## The Starving Crowd (Market Selection for Offers)

Before designing an offer, verify the market:

| Criterion | Question | Must-Have? |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Massive Pain | Are they in acute, urgent pain RIGHT NOW? | Yes |
| Purchasing Power | Can they actually afford it? | Yes |
| Easy to Target | Can you find and reach them efficiently? | Yes |
| Growing Market | Is the market expanding? | Strongly preferred |

**Priority:** Starving Crowd > Offer Strength > Sales Skill. A mediocre offer to a desperate market beats a brilliant offer to an indifferent one.

## Grand Slam Offer Creation (6 Steps)

### Step 1: Identify the Dream Outcome
State it in THEIR words, specific and measurable.
- Not: "Grow your business"
- Yes: "Add $50K/month in recurring revenue within 90 days"

### Step 2: List All Obstacles (20+ minimum)
Every problem, objection, fear, and barrier. Include problems BEFORE, DURING, and AFTER achieving the outcome.

### Step 3: Turn Obstacles into Solutions
Add "How to..." and reverse the problem:
- Problem: "I don't have time to cook healthy"
- Solution: "How to eat healthy in 10 minutes with pre-made meal plans"

### Step 4: Create Delivery Vehicles
For each solution, pick HOW to deliver:

| Vehicle | Value | Cost |
|---------|-------|------|
| Done-for-you | Highest | Highest |
| 1-on-1 | High | High |
| Small group | Medium | Medium |
| Course / 1-to-many | Scalable | Lower |
| DIY + tools | Lowest | Lowest |

### Step 5: Trim & Stack
Plot on a 2x2 (Cost to Deliver vs. Value to Customer):
- **KEEP:** Low cost + High value (profit maximizers)
- **KEEP:** High cost + High value (differentiators)
- **CUT:** Everything else

Stack remaining items into one cohesive offer.

### Step 6: Name Using M.A.G.I.C.
- **M**ake a magnetic promise
- **A**nnounce the avatar (target customer)
- **G**ive a timeline
- **I**ndicate the container (format)
- **C**reate curiosity

Example: "The 6-Week Lean Body Accelerator for Busy Executives"

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Company-level:** Industry, size, revenue, tech stack, geography
**Persona-level:** Title, department, pain points, buying authority

**Build from your best customers:**
1. Study your top 10 happiest, highest-LTV customers
2. What do they have in common?
3. That commonality IS your ICP
4. ICP is NOT "anyone who might buy" — it's the profile that converts AND retains best

## Pricing Models

| Model | Best For | Risk |
|-------|---------|------|
| Subscription (flat) | Predictable products, clear value | Churn if value perception drops |
| Tiered | Products with clear usage segments | Customers gaming the cheapest tier |
| Usage-based | Variable consumption products | Revenue unpredictability |
| Per-seat | Collaboration tools | Seat-stuffing, sharing logins |
| Outcome-based | High-trust relationships | Dispute over outcome measurement |
| Freemium | PLG, network effects | Free users never converting |

### Pricing Rules (Hormozi)
1. Never price based on cost-plus — price based on VALUE of the dream outcome
2. Never price based on competitors — your Grand Slam Offer should be incomparable
3. If no one says "that's expensive," your price is too low
4. If close rate is above 30-40%, you're priced too low
5. Price at 10-20% of the value delivered (5-10x ROI for the customer)

### The Virtuous Price Cycle
```
Higher Price -> Higher perceived value -> More committed customers
-> Better results -> More testimonials -> Higher perceived likelihood
-> Even higher value -> Can raise price again
```

### Rob's Rule of 10 (Existing Customer Pricing)
If raising prices won't grow MRR by at least 10%, don't do it. Grandfather existing users. Apply new pricing to new customers only.

## Risk Reversal & Guarantees

| Type | What It Is | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Unconditional | "30-day money-back, no questions asked" | Low-ticket, digital products |
| Conditional | "Complete all modules, no results = full refund" | High-ticket, requires customer action |
| Anti-Guarantee | "All sales final. We're that confident." | Premium/luxury, exclusive access |
| Performance | "We take 10% of revenue we generate" | Services, done-for-you |

**Stack guarantees:** Unconditional (30-day) + Conditional (90-day results-based) addresses both "what if I don't like it?" AND "what if it doesn't work?"

## Enhancing the Offer

### Scarcity (quantity-based)
- Limited spots, limited bonuses, limited access
- MUST be real — fake scarcity destroys trust permanently

### Urgency (time-based)
- Cohort-based, seasonal, decision deadlines
- Never without a genuine reason

### Bonuses
- Solve adjacent problems
- Each has standalone dollar value
- Low cost to you, high perceived value
- Total bonus value should exceed core offer price

---

## The "Too Good to Refuse" Checklist

Before launching any offer:

- [ ] Dream Outcome is specific, emotional, measurable?
- [ ] Prospect believes they'll achieve it? (proof + guarantee)
- [ ] Time to result is as short as possible?
- [ ] Effort required is minimal or clearly guided?
- [ ] Price is less than 1/10th the value?
- [ ] Real scarcity and urgency constraints?
- [ ] Bonuses solve adjacent problems?
- [ ] Guarantee removes ALL buyer risk?
- [ ] Offer is impossible to compare to competitors?
- [ ] Would YOU feel stupid saying no?
