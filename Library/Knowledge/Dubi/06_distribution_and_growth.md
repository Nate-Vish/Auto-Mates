# Topic 6: Distribution & Growth

*Distribution is the product. Build channels before features.*

## The 19 Traction Channels (Weinberg & Mares)

| # | Channel | Description |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Viral Marketing | Built-in product mechanics (invite loops, sharing, network effects) |
| 2 | PR | Earned media — newspapers, magazines, online publications |
| 3 | Unconventional PR | Publicity stunts, remarkable customer service stories |
| 4 | SEM | Paid search ads (buying intent) |
| 5 | Social & Display Ads | Paid social (buying attention) |
| 6 | Offline Ads | TV, radio, billboards, direct mail |
| 7 | SEO | Organic search rankings (slow, high-intent) |
| 8 | Content Marketing | Blog, video, podcast — attract target audience |
| 9 | Email Marketing | Drip campaigns, newsletters, sequences |
| 10 | Engineering as Marketing | Free tools, calculators, widgets that attract prospects |
| 11 | Targeting Blogs | Niche communities, forums, influencer blogs |
| 12 | Business Development | Strategic partnerships, distribution deals, integrations |
| 13 | Sales | Direct outreach to prospects (high-touch, B2B) |
| 14 | Affiliate Programs | Commission-based referrals |
| 15 | Existing Platforms | App stores, Shopify, Salesforce marketplace |
| 16 | Trade Shows | Industry events, demos, face-to-face |
| 17 | Offline Events | Meetups, conferences, dinners, workshops |
| 18 | Speaking Engagements | Conferences, panels, podcasts |
| 19 | Community Building | Forums, Discord, Slack groups, user communities |

**Before committing to ANY marketing plan:** Brainstorm one concrete idea for each of the 19 channels. The winner is often NOT the one you'd naturally gravitate toward.

## The Bullseye Framework (Channel Prioritization)

### Step 1: Brainstorm (Outer Ring)
One idea per channel, all 19. Don't dismiss any.

### Step 2: Rank
Score each 1-5 on: cost, scalability, targeting, time to results, audience fit.

### Step 3: Prioritize (Middle Ring)
Promote top 3-6 channels for testing.

### Step 4: Test (Inner Ring)
For each Middle Ring channel, run a cheap, fast test answering THREE questions:
1. **Acquisition Cost:** How much per customer?
2. **Channel Volume:** How many customers available?
3. **Customer Quality:** Are these the RIGHT customers?

### Step 5: Focus
Once you find a channel that works: **STOP testing others.** Go all-in on your Bullseye. Only add a second channel when the first is fully optimized or saturated.

## The 50% Rule

Spend 50% of your time on product, 50% on traction/distribution. From day one.

"Almost every failed startup has a product. What failed startups don't have is traction."

Build product and distribution in parallel, not sequentially. Traction informs product decisions.

## Organic vs. Paid Split

| Stage | Organic Focus | Paid Focus |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| Pre-PMF | 80% (content, community, SEO groundwork) | 20% (small tests to validate channels) |
| Post-PMF, pre-scale | 50% | 50% (scale what works) |
| Scaling | 30% (maintain) | 70% (accelerate proven channels) |

**Organic advantages:** Compounds over time, lower CAC, builds trust
**Paid advantages:** Immediate results, scalable, measurable

## Growth Models: PLG vs. SLG vs. CLG

| Model | How It Works | Best For | Key Metric |
|-------|-------------|---------|------------|
| **PLG** (Product-Led) | Product sells itself — free trial, freemium, self-serve onboarding | Low-price SaaS ($10-500/mo), collaboration tools | Activation rate, time-to-value |
| **SLG** (Sales-Led) | Sales team demos, qualifies, closes | High-price B2B ($500-10K+/mo), enterprise | Pipeline velocity, close rate |
| **CLG** (Content-Led) | Content attracts and educates audience, builds trust, generates leads | Thought leadership, education-first products | SEO traffic, lead quality |

### Three SaaS Funnels (Walling)

| Funnel | Price Point | Motion |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Low-Touch | $10-500/mo | Self-serve. Free trial. No sales calls. |
| High-Touch | $500-10K+/mo | Demo calls. Dedicated onboarding. Account managers. |
| Dual | Both | Self-serve for small, sales for enterprise. Two motions. |

Start with ONE funnel. Master it before adding the second.

## Partnerships & Business Development (Gal's addition)

Often overlooked by technical founders. BD is channel #12 in Traction but can be the most leveraged.

### Types of BD Partnerships

| Type | What It Is | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Integration partnerships | Your product plugs into theirs | Zapier integrations, API partnerships |
| Distribution partnerships | They sell/promote your product to their audience | Reseller agreements, marketplace listings |
| Co-marketing | Joint content, webinars, case studies | Mutual audience exposure |
| Strategic alliances | Deep product alignment, co-development | Platform partnerships |
| Channel partnerships | Third parties sell your product for commission | VAR (Value Added Reseller) |

### BD Qualification
Before pursuing a partnership, verify:
- Do they reach YOUR ICP?
- Is the value exchange balanced? (What do they get?)
- Can you measure the results?
- Is there a champion inside their organization?

---

## Leverage Plays (Multiply Reach Without Proportional Effort)

1. **Marketplace listings** — one integration, ongoing leads (Shopify, Salesforce AppExchange)
2. **Engineering as marketing** — build a free tool that attracts your ICP (calculators, templates, analyzers)
3. **Content SEO** — one article ranking #1 generates leads for years
4. **Referral mechanics** — each customer brings 1+ new customer (viral coefficient > 1)
5. **Community** — members help each other (reduces support cost, increases retention)
6. **Open source** — code is free, services are paid (builds trust, creates ecosystem)

## Technology Adoption Lifecycle (Moore)

| Segment | % of Market | What They Buy | How They Decide |
|---------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Innovators | ~2.5% | The technology itself | "Is it cool? Is it new?" |
| Early Adopters | ~13.5% | A vision of transformation | "Can this give me 10x advantage?" |
| Early Majority | ~34% | Proven, complete solutions | "Who else like me is using this?" |
| Late Majority | ~34% | Convenience and necessity | "Is it safe? Is it the standard?" |
| Laggards | ~16% | Only when forced | "I don't want this." |

**The Chasm:** Between Early Adopters and Early Majority. Different buyer psychology, different sales process, different product completeness requirements. Most tech companies die here.

**Crossing it:** Pick ONE narrow beachhead segment, commit ALL resources, win it completely, then expand.
