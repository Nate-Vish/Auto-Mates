# Topic 10: Scaling & Team Building

*Stabilize -> Automate -> Delegate -> Scale. In that order.*

---

## The Four Scaling Phases

| Phase | Focus | You're Ready When |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| **1. Stabilize** | Product works, customers exist, unit economics positive | Consistent positive unit economics for 3+ months |
| **2. Automate** | Remove yourself from repetitive processes | SOPs documented, key processes running without you daily |
| **3. Delegate** | Hire people to run the systems you built | New hire can follow SOPs and produce consistent results within 30 days |
| **4. Scale** | Pour fuel on what works — expand channels, markets, team | Revenue grows faster than headcount. Gross margin maintained. |

**The trap:** Jumping to Phase 4 before completing Phases 1-3. Scaling a broken process just breaks it faster and louder.

## Hiring Order by Business Model

### SaaS Hiring Sequence (Walling)

| Hire # | Role | Why First |
|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | **Support / Customer Success** | Frees founder to work ON the product instead of answering tickets |
| 2 | **Marketing / Growth** | Founder can build but usually can't scale distribution alone |
| 3 | **Engineering** | Once you know WHAT to build (validated), hire to build faster |
| 4 | **Sales** | Only if high-touch funnel. Not needed for self-serve. |
| 5 | **Operations (HR, Legal, Finance)** | Defer as long as possible. Use contractors and tools. |

### General Startup Sequence

| Stage | Key Hires | Avoid |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| $0-100K ARR | Co-founder or first generalist | Specialists, executives |
| $100K-500K ARR | Support + marketing/growth | Full-time ops roles |
| $500K-2M ARR | Engineering + sales (if needed) | VP-level titles |
| $2M+ ARR | Department leads, ops | Hiring ahead of revenue |

### When to Add a Co-Founder vs. Hire vs. Outsource

| Need | Co-Founder | Hire | Outsource |
|------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Core technical skill you lack | If pre-revenue, full-time commitment needed | If revenue exists to pay salary | If project-based, time-boxed |
| Second perspective on strategy | Yes — if you trust them | No (employees don't share strategic burden) | No |
| Capacity to ship faster | Only if it's a permanent need | Yes | If temporary surge |
| Domain expertise you need | If they bring network + credibility | If ongoing | If one-time learning |

**Co-founder red flags:** Adding one to "split the work" (not a strategic reason), different risk tolerances, no vesting agreement, can't have hard conversations.

## Role Definition & Job Descriptions

### Position Contract (Gerber)

For every role (even if YOU fill it today):

```
Role: [Title]
Reports to: [Position]

Accountabilities:
1. [Specific outcome this role is responsible for]
2. [Specific outcome]
3. [Specific outcome]

Standards:
- [Measurable quality bar for accountability #1]
- [Measurable quality bar for accountability #2]

Work Description:
- [Daily/weekly activities]
- [Tools and systems used]
- [Handoff points with other roles]
```

**Key principle:** Define ROLES, not tasks. Each hire should OWN a function.

### Hiring Trigger
Hire when the pain of NOT having someone in the role is **limiting growth**, not when you feel overwhelmed (overwhelm is normal).

## Leading Metrics per Scale Phase

| Phase | Metrics to Watch | Danger Signal |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Stabilize** | Churn rate, CAC payback, activation rate | Churn increasing, CAC payback > 18 months |
| **Automate** | Time-to-deliver, error rate, process completion % | Manual intervention still required for automated processes |
| **Delegate** | Ramp time for new hires, output consistency | New hire takes 6+ months to be productive |
| **Scale** | Revenue per employee, gross margin %, burn multiple | Revenue grows slower than headcount |

### Vanity Metrics to Ignore
- Total registered users (only goes up)
- Total page views (no context)
- Social media followers (vanity)
- Total downloads (doesn't mean usage)

### Metrics That Matter
- Revenue per employee
- Gross margin trend
- NRR (Net Revenue Retention)
- CAC trend (should be stable or decreasing)
- Burn multiple (under 2x)

## Bottleneck Prediction at Phase Transitions

| Transition | Common Bottleneck | Prevention |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Stabilize -> Automate | Founder doesn't document processes | Treat SOPs as a product. Time-box documentation weekly. |
| Automate -> Delegate | "Nobody can do it like me" | The Franchise Prototype mindset. Lowest-skill-level systems. |
| Delegate -> Scale | Team can't make decisions without founder | Decision authority matrix. Clear escalation rules. |
| Scale -> Mature | Culture dilutes with rapid hiring | Values documented. Hiring for culture fit + skill. |

## Scaling Checklist

Before pouring fuel on growth, verify:

- [ ] Unit economics are positive (LTV:CAC > 3:1)
- [ ] Churn is stable or decreasing
- [ ] At least one acquisition channel is predictable and measurable
- [ ] Key processes are documented (SOPs exist)
- [ ] New hires can ramp within 30-90 days using documentation
- [ ] Revenue grows faster than headcount
- [ ] Gross margin is maintained as you scale
- [ ] Customer quality is consistent (not degrading with volume)
- [ ] You've been at this stability for 3+ months (not one good month)

## The Bowling Alley Strategy (Moore)

After winning your beachhead market segment:

1. **Win Pin 1 completely** — dominate so thoroughly that adjacent segments hear about you
2. **Pick Pin 2** — the segment most similar to Pin 1 (shared pain, similar buyer, overlapping product needs)
3. **Adapt, don't rebuild** — extend product for new segment, don't start over
4. **Repeat** — each pin creates social proof for the next
5. **Watch for the tornado** — at some point, mainstream adoption becomes self-reinforcing

## The Whole Product Model (Moore)

What you need to deliver at scale:

| Layer | What It Is | Example (CRM) |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **Generic Product** | What ships in the box | The CRM application |
| **Expected Product** | What customers assume is included | Onboarding, support, data import, uptime SLA |
| **Augmented Product** | What adds differentiation | API integrations, training, community, custom reports |
| **Potential Product** | Future capabilities | AI features, marketplace, platform extensibility |

**Pre-chasm:** Generic product is enough (early adopters assemble the rest)
**Post-chasm:** Must deliver Expected + Augmented (pragmatists won't assemble anything)

## Israeli Startup Scaling Patterns

### Born Global Imperative
Israel's domestic market (9.3M people) is too small. Every Israeli startup must:
- Architect for international markets from day one
- Establish US sales within 6-18 months
- Build R&D in Israel, sales in target market

### Enterprise-First Pattern
- 78% of Israeli tech startups are B2B
- Products built for CIO/CTO buyers from inception
- SOC2/ISO 27001 compliance prioritized early
- Focus: problems enterprise will pay premium for

### Military-to-Startup Pipeline
- 20%+ of founders from elite IDF tech units
- Dense alumni networks fuel co-founding teams
- Skills transfer: intelligence analysis -> market analysis
- Flat hierarchy culture: junior people challenge senior people
