## TAM/SAM/SOM (Bottom-Up, Not Fantasy)

| Level | What It Is | How to Calculate | Red Flag |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | Everyone who could theoretically buy | Industry reports, analyst data | Using TAM in a pitch as if you'll capture it |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | The segment you can actually reach | TAM filtered by geography, language, channel, price point | SAM = TAM (means you haven't filtered) |
| **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | What you'll realistically capture in 1-3 years | Bottom-up: (reachable prospects) x (conversion rate) x (price) | "We only need 1% of the market" |

**The 1% Fallacy:** "If we capture just 1% of a $10B market..." is a red flag, not a strategy. It means you have no idea how you'll get customers. Always calculate bottom-up.

**Bottom-Up Formula:**
```
SOM = (Prospects you can actually reach in Year 1) x (Realistic conversion rate) x (Average deal size)
```

Example: 5,000 reachable companies x 2% close rate x $500/mo x 12 = $600K ARR Year 1.

## Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

People don't buy products. They hire them to do a job.

**The JTBD Statement:**
```
When I [situation/trigger], I want to [motivation/job], so I can [expected outcome].
```

**How to discover jobs:**
1. Interview customers who recently switched TO or FROM a solution
2. Ask: "What were you trying to accomplish?" (not "what features do you want?")
3. Map the forces: Push (pain with current), Pull (attraction of new), Anxiety (fear of switching), Habit (comfort with current)
4. The job is stable even when solutions change (people needed to "capture memories" long before smartphones)

**When JTBD fails:** When the job is vague ("make my life easier") or when customers don't recognize they have the job until you show them the solution (demand creation, not demand discovery).

## Demand Signal Sources

Where to find evidence that a market exists BEFORE building:

| Source | What to Look For | Tool |
|--------|-----------------|------|
| **Search volume** | People actively searching for solutions | Google Trends, Ahrefs, SEMrush |
| **Forum complaints** | Unresolved pain points | Reddit, Quora, Stack Overflow, niche forums |
| **Review complaints** | What competitors' users hate | G2, Capterra, App Store, Trustpilot |
| **Job postings** | Companies hiring to solve the problem manually | LinkedIn, Indeed |
| **VC thesis** | Investors betting on the space | Crunchbase, VC blogs, thesis posts |
| **Regulatory changes** | New requirements creating new needs | Government sites, industry publications |
| **Conference talks** | What practitioners are struggling with | YouTube, conference agendas |

**Strong signal:** Multiple sources pointing at the same pain.
**Weak signal:** Only one source, or only VCs talking about it (supply-side hype).

## Trend Analysis & Niche Identification

### Underserved vs. Saturated Detection

| Signal | Underserved Market | Saturated Market |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Competitors | Few, or none addressing the specific pain | 10+ funded players with similar positioning |
| Reviews | "I wish it did X" complaints common | Satisfied users, feature-complete products |
| Pricing | Customers paying premium for mediocre solutions | Race to the bottom, feature parity |
| Switching | High willingness to try new solutions | Entrenched incumbents with high switching costs |
| Community | Active forums asking for solutions | Quiet — problem is "solved enough" |

### Market Timing Framework (Thiel's Timing Question)

Ask: "Why NOW? Why not 5 years ago? Why not 5 years from now?"

Good timing signals:
- Technology shift just made something possible (AI, mobile, API economy)
- Regulation just created new requirements (GDPR, EU AI Act)
- Cultural shift changed behavior (remote work, creator economy)
- Cost curve just crossed a threshold (cloud compute, bandwidth)

Bad timing: "This has always been needed" = no urgency trigger.

## The Idea Maze (Srinivasan/Dixon)

A good startup idea isn't just an entrance to a maze — it's a map showing which turns lead to treasure and which to death.

**Key test:** Can you explain:
1. Why previous attempts in this space failed?
2. What changed to make your approach viable now?
3. What happens if competitors copy you? (Your next moves are planned)
4. What happens if the market shifts? (You have contingency paths)

**Red flag:** Founder runs into the maze ("photo sharing!") without knowing the history, players, casualties, or technology shifts.

## Founder-Market Fit Assessment

Before committing, score yourself honestly:

| Criterion | Score 1-5 | What "5" Looks Like |
|-----------|-----------|--------------------|
| Domain expertise | | Worked in the industry, know the workflows and language |
| Network access | | Can reach 10-20 potential customers through warm intros |
| Technical capability | | Can build v1 without hiring a dev team |
| Passion/endurance | | Would work on this for 5-10 years |
| Unfair insight | | Know something about this market competitors don't |

**Below 3 on three or more:** Reconsider the market. Strongest signal: "I had this problem myself, built a solution, and others want it too."
