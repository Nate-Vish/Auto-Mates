# Topic 7: Sales Systems & Process

*Sales cures all. Build a pipeline, not a hope machine.*

---

## Sales Role Specialization (Aaron Ross)

The biggest productivity killer: one person doing prospecting, qualifying, closing, AND account management. Split into three roles.

| Role | Function | Key Metric | Does NOT |
|------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **SDR** (Sales Development Rep) | Generate qualified pipeline from cold/inactive accounts | Qualified opps created/month (target: 8-12) | Close deals or manage accounts |
| **AE** (Account Executive) | Close deals from qualified opportunities | Closed-won revenue/quarter | Prospect for new leads |
| **CSM** (Customer Success Manager) | Onboarding, retention, expansion, renewals | Net retention rate, expansion revenue | Hunt for new business |

**When to specialize:**
- Under $1M ARR: Split prospecting from closing (2 functions in 1-2 people)
- 10+ active customers: Add CSM function
- Never let AEs prospect — it's the most expensive prospecting you can do

## Cold Calling 2.0 (Ross)

**NOT cold calling.** It's cold email -> referral -> warm call.

### The Process
1. **Build ICP** — study your best 10 customers, find the pattern
2. **Build target list** — companies and contacts matching ICP
3. **Send referral emails** — to executives ABOVE your target buyer
4. **Follow up** — 2-3 times over 2-3 weeks, then stop
5. **Qualify and hand off** — SDR qualifies, passes to AE

### The Referral Email Template

```
Subject: [Company Name] -- quick question

Hi [First Name],

I'm not sure if you're the right person to speak with about
[specific topic] at [Company], but I was hoping you could
point me to the right person.

We help [companies like theirs] with [specific value prop
in 1 sentence].

Would you mind pointing me to the right person?

Thanks,
[Name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Executives feel respected (not pitched, asked for help)
- Referral comes with built-in credibility
- 7-10% response rate vs. 1-3% for cold pitches

### Email Cadence
- Day 1: Initial referral email
- Day 3-4: First follow-up (shorter, different angle)
- Day 7-8: Second follow-up (add value or insight)
- Day 14: Final follow-up ("closing the loop")
- STOP after 4 touches. Move to next batch.

## Three Types of Leads: Seeds, Nets, Spears

| Type | Source | Conversion | Cost | Quality |
|------|--------|-----------|------|---------|
| **Seeds** | Referrals, word-of-mouth, organic | Highest | Lowest (but slow to build) | Best |
| **Nets** | Inbound marketing (SEO, content, ads) | Medium | Medium | Mixed (needs qualification) |
| **Spears** | Outbound prospecting (Cold Calling 2.0) | Lower but targeted | Highest | High per lead |

### Lead Mix by Stage

| Stage | Seeds | Nets | Spears |
|-------|-------|------|--------|
| Pre-Revenue / MVP | 80% | 10% | 10% |
| $0-1M ARR | 40% | 20% | 40% |
| $1-10M ARR | 30% | 30% | 40% |
| $10M+ ARR | 20% | 40% | 40% |

**Danger:** Over-reliant on Seeds (>60%) = vulnerable to one bad quarter. Over-reliant on Spears (>60%) = CAC too high.

## Pipeline Stages & Entry/Exit Criteria

| Stage | Entry Criteria | Exit Criteria |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| **Lead** | Matches ICP, contact info verified | Responded to outreach OR inbound inquiry |
| **Qualified** | Confirmed need, budget exists, authority identified | Discovery call completed, pain confirmed |
| **Discovery** | Detailed understanding of problem, stakeholders mapped | Demo/proposal requested |
| **Proposal** | Custom proposal or demo delivered | Decision timeline confirmed |
| **Negotiation** | Terms discussed, procurement involved | Verbal agreement or clear "no" |
| **Closed-Won** | Contract signed, payment received | Handoff to CSM |
| **Closed-Lost** | Clear "no" with documented reason | Post-mortem logged |

## Qualification Framework

### BANT (Classic)
- **B**udget: Can they afford it?
- **A**uthority: Can this person decide?
- **N**eed: Do they have the problem?
- **T**imeline: When do they need to solve it?

### MEDDIC (Enterprise)
- **M**etrics: What quantifiable outcomes do they need?
- **E**conomic Buyer: Who signs the check?
- **D**ecision Criteria: How will they evaluate?
- **D**ecision Process: What are the steps to buy?
- **I**dentify Pain: What's the cost of inaction?
- **C**hampion: Who inside will fight for you?

## Objection Handling

Don't argue. Reframe.

| Objection | What They Mean | Reframe |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| "It's too expensive" | They don't see the value yet | "What's it costing you NOT to solve this?" |
| "We need to think about it" | Not enough urgency or clarity | "What specifically do you need to think through?" |
| "We're using [competitor]" | Switching cost concern | "What made you start looking for alternatives?" |
| "We'll build it internally" | Control + customization need | "How long would that take? What's the opportunity cost?" |
| "Not the right time" | Other priorities competing | "When would be the right time? What needs to change?" |
| "I need to check with my team" | Not the decision-maker | "Happy to. Who else should be involved? Can I join that conversation?" |

## Closing Sequences

### The Follow-Up Cadence
- After proposal: Follow up within 24 hours (clarify questions)
- Day 3: Add value (case study, ROI calculator, relevant insight)
- Day 7: Check in on timeline
- Day 14: Direct ask ("Are we moving forward?")
- Day 21: Break-up email ("I'll close this out unless I hear from you")

### Closing Signals
- Asking about implementation timeline
- Introducing you to other stakeholders
- Asking about contract terms
- Requesting references

## Founder-Led Sales vs. Hiring

### Stay Founder-Led When:
- You're under $1M ARR
- You haven't documented a repeatable sales process
- Close rate varies wildly deal-to-deal
- You can't articulate exactly why customers buy

### Hire Sales When:
- You've closed 20-30+ deals personally
- You can describe the process a new hire would follow
- Close rates are consistent and predictable
- Response rates and conversion rates are documented

### The Predictable Revenue Formula
```
Predictable Leads -> Predictable Pipeline -> Predictable Revenue
```
```
Emails x Response Rate x Qualification Rate x Close Rate x Deal Size = Revenue
```

**Pipeline coverage ratio:** 3:1 (need 3x quota in pipeline to reliably hit targets).

## Measuring Sales Health

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Emails sent per SDR/day | 50-100 |
| Response rate | 7-10% |
| Conversations per week | 15-25 |
| Qualified opps per SDR/month | 8-12 |
| AE acceptance of SDR opps | 80%+ |
| Opportunity-to-close rate | 20-30% |
| Sales cycle (mid-market SaaS) | 30-90 days |
| Pipeline coverage | 3:1 |

## Quarterly Sales Diagnostic

- [ ] SDRs, AEs, CSMs in separate roles with separate metrics?
- [ ] ICP documented and validated against best customers?
- [ ] Tracking Seeds, Nets, and Spears separately?
- [ ] Response rate, qualification rate, close rate known?
- [ ] Pipeline coverage at least 3:1?
- [ ] New SDR can ramp within 3 months using playbook?
- [ ] AEs accepting 80%+ of SDR-qualified opportunities?
- [ ] Revenue forecast within 15% accuracy?
